项目主旨

本项目旨在开发一款综合型校园App，提供一个统一、高效的数字平台，为学生打造一站式校园生活解决方案。

项目提案

研究问题/需求分析

基于10位计算机专业学生问卷和4位深度访谈，发现校园服务存在三大核心问题：

  • 信息碎片化：85%受访者反映课程、活动、通知分散在不同平台
  • 系统不互通：70%用户遭遇数据同步问题（如课程表与日历分离）
  • 功能体验差：导航功能缺失、通知过载、界面复杂

研究目标与假设

核心目标：设计整合型校园App，实现：

  • 三合一平台：课程管理+校园服务+社交互动
  • 自动化同步：100%课程数据自动同步
  • 智能通知：分级消息推送系统
核心假设：统一平台可使任务处理效率提升40%（问卷显示用户平均满意度仅2.8/5）

方法论

  1. 定性研究：4场半结构化访谈，聚焦：
    • 用户旅程地图绘制
    • 功能优先级排序
  2. 定量验证：扩展问卷至30+样本：
    • 5点量表题：评估8大功能需求强度
    • 开放式问题：收集具体场景痛点

数据整理与分析

（一）Survey数据分析报告

本次调查共收集了10位有效样本（用户A-J），以下是详细的人口统计学特征描述：

人口统计学特征描述

  • 样本规模：N = 10
  • 专业分布：全部10位受访者（100%）的专业均为计算机科学与技术。具有高度的专业同质性，其结果能很好地反映该特定学生群体的需求和痛点。
  • 年级分布：
    • 大一：1人 (10%)
    • 大二：2人 (20%)
    • 大三：3人 (30%)
    • 大四：2人 (20%)
    • 研一：2人 (20%)

    分析：样本覆盖了从本科到研究生各个年级，分布较为均匀，能够捕捉不同学习阶段学生的可能差异。

  • 应用使用频率：
    • 每日使用：4人 (A, C, F, H)，占比40%
    • 每周使用：6人 (B, D, E, G, I, J)，占比60%

    分析：大部分用户（60%）为每周使用，但每日使用者也占相当比例（40%），表明现有平台是学生日常学习生活中不可或缺的工具，用户基础活跃。

核心指标量化分析

  1. 满意度与使用情况评分统计
    • 日程满意度平均分：3.1/5（最高4分，最低2分）
    • 平台使用频率平均分：4.3/5（最高5分，最低3分）
    • 活动信息获取难度：2.9/5（分数越高越难）
    • 导航功能使用意愿：4.6/5（所有指标中最高）
    • 工具有效性评分：3.4/5
    • 简洁设计重要性：4.8/5（10人中有8人评为5分）
  2. 对"最期望功能"进行词频统计发现：
    • 作业跟踪（assignment tracker）：被提及7次
    • 成绩查询（grade overview）：被提及6次
    • 资源库（resource library）：被提及6次
    • 考试提醒（exam reminders）：被提及5次
    • 课表（class schedule）：被提及4次
  3. 期望整合的服务排名：
    • 活动公告（event announcements）：被提及7次
    • 校园地图（campus map）：被提及6次
    • 公交时刻表（bus schedule）：被提及6次
    • 食堂菜单（cafeteria menu）：被提及5次
    • 失物招领（lost & found）：被提及3次

深入的数据洞察与发现

  1. 使用模式与满意度关联分析

    数据显示，每日使用者（A、C、F、H）的日程满意度平均分为3.5，高于每周使用者（B、D、E、G、I、J）的平均分2.8。这表明高频使用者对现有系统有相对较高的适应性，但整体满意度仍处于中等偏下水平。

  2. 年级与需求差异分析
    • 低年级学生（大一、大二）更关注"课表"和"作业跟踪"等基础功能
    • 高年级学生（大三、大四、研究生）对"资源库"和"成绩查询"需求更强烈
    • 研究生用户对"活动公告"和"校园导航"的整合需求明显高于本科生
  3. 工具使用与信息遗漏关联

    数据分析显示，使用单一工具（仅微信）的用户中，信息遗漏率达到67%（4/6），而使用组合工具（微信+邮件/LINE）的用户遗漏率为25%（1/4）。这表明工具多样性有助于降低信息遗漏风险。

核心发现总结

数据显示，核心用户画像：具备一定技术背景、深度依赖数字工具进行学习管理的年轻群体。他们的主要需求高度集中在课程管理（如作业跟踪、成绩查询）和校园资源整合（如活动公告、校园导航）两大类别。

  1. "高频"与"低满意"并存：尽管用户使用现有平台很频繁（平均分4.3/5），但对日程管理的满意度却仅为中等水平（平均分3.1/5）。这表明用户可能存在"被动依赖"，当前工具并非因为他们非常满意，而是暂时没有更好的选择。
  2. 对"一站式整合"的渴望超出预期：用户对导航整合功能表现出极高的意愿（平均分4.6/5），是所有评分中最高的。这强烈暗示，用户期望的应用不仅仅是一个工具，更是一个能解决校园信息孤岛问题的"统一门户"。
  3. 现有活动组织工具存在明显短板：尽管微信是组织活动的主要工具，但其有效性评分一般（平均分3.4/5），且有高达60%的用户曾因此错过重要信息。这揭示了通用社交工具在特定场景下的不适应性，以及一个专注于校园活动组织的可靠工具存在的市场缺口。

优化方向启示

这些发现为产品优化指明了方向：首要任务是解决信息碎片化问题，强化平台的一站式整合能力；同时，应高度重视操作的简洁性设计；此外，开发或优化专门的活动组织模块，弥补现有工具的不足，也显得尤为重要。

综合分析与主要发现（Synthesis and Findings）

  1. 核心需求：统一整合的校园App生态系统

    所有受访者均希望将课程、活动、任务、通知集中在一个平台中管理。当前信息分散（官网、邮件、群聊、独立App），效率低。

  2. 学习管理功能需智能化与自动化

    自动同步课程信息与任务截止日期是学生的主要痛点。进阶功能如"学习计划、进度分析、表现评估"有较高兴趣。

  3. 校园信息服务应一体化与可视化

    导航地图、活动整合、资源借用等服务应集成。强调"校园数字化助手"概念。

  4. 交流方式需去噪与结构化

    希望摆脱群聊杂乱的信息，使用结构化的"公告区"和"讨论区"。支持项目协作与师生互动模块。

  5. 设计层面强调可用性与简洁性

    学生普遍关注界面设计、信息层级与使用体验。"简洁、美观、逻辑清晰"是核心设计原则。

Critique Feedback

一、 Overall Tone of the Critique Feedback

The overall tone of the critique feedback is constructive, collaborative, and highly analytical. Peers provided their comments with the intention of strengthening the structure and clarity of the wireframe, rather than simply pointing out flaws. Reviewers noted that the current design presents a clear functional concept for a "本科毕业设计管理系统" and effectively covers the main workflows such as topic selection, proposal submission, instructor feedback, and training resources. The feedback reflects a shared understanding that the project is well-scoped, supported by consistent navigation, and demonstrates a logical page-to-page progression.

At the same time, peers expressed that some organizational aspects of the wireframe could be refined to ensure a smoother user experience. They highlighted gaps in information hierarchy, task guidance, and page clarity, especially for users interacting with the system for the first time. Overall, the critique is encouraging but also points to meaningful opportunities for refinement. The tone emphasizes improvement over criticism, and peers consistently give actionable insights that help push the wireframe toward a more professional and user-centered direction.

二、 Problems Identified by Peers

Category A - Information Architecture & Clarity

This category contains the most frequently mentioned issues, indicating its central importance. Peers pointed out that several pages contain large blocks of content without visible hierarchy, making it hard for users to quickly understand the relationships between different sections. For instance, pages such as "动态" and "写作培训材料" present multiple types of information, but the hierarchy between titles, secondary titles, and content is not clearly communicated. Reviewers also raised concerns that the purpose of certain sections is not obvious, especially to new users.

Additionally, users may feel unsure about where to start or what the system expects them to do next, due to the lack of introductory guidance or contextual cues. This led to questions about the system's ability to support first-time users and ensure smooth onboarding.

Category B - Navigation & Interaction Flow

Reviewers agreed that while the bottom navigation bar is consistent, the interaction logic between pages is not always intuitive. For example, transitions between "主页-任务-动态-我的" seem functional but lack visible indications of user progress or the relationship between stages such as "选题-开题-中期-答辩".

Some peers noted that detail pages have no clear return paths, such as when viewing comments ("查看批注"), or opening topic details in the "选题大厅". This raised questions about the overall flow and what the user should expect after performing each action.

Furthermore, some interactions feel incomplete or ambiguous, such as how users proceed after submitting a document, or how they revisit past conversations with instructors. These concerns reflect a need to strengthen the consistency and predictability of user interactions.

Category C - Visual Layout & Consistency

Though not as critical as the first two categories, peers highlighted several layout-related concerns. These include inconsistent spacing, varying alignment of text blocks, and an occasional mismatch between icons and labels. Reviewers noted that such inconsistencies may distract users or reduce readability, especially on pages containing multiple functional modules.

Several peers suggested that uneven visual structure affects the perception of professionalism and coherence, highlighting the importance of consistent layout standards across all wireframe pages.

三、 Response to Each Problem

Category A - Information Architecture & Clarity

The primary issue stems from an insufficient application of visual hierarchy and a lack of contextual guidance for new users. To address the lack of hierarchy, the core solution is to break down large content blocks (e.g., in "动态" and "写作培训材料") into discrete card-based modules with clear boundaries. We will implement distinct font weights and sizes for H1 and H2 headings, ensuring consistent vertical spacing to visually communicate the relationship between titles and their corresponding content. To resolve the concern about unclear purpose and onboarding, we will introduce a concise, non-intrusive modal guide upon first visit to key task pages. Furthermore, essential actions will be highlighted using highly contrasting Primary Buttons (Call to Action, CTA), and every task flow will feature a progress indicator or timeline to unambiguously show the user's current location and the system's expected next step. This plan directly targets the usability gap and clarifies information priority.

Category B - Navigation & Interaction Flow

The critique in this category highlights a lack of system feedback regarding user state and transition paths. To resolve the lack of user progress visualization, the "任务" and "主页" sections will integrate a Multi-Step Stepper Component. This component will visually map out the four academic stages (Topic Selection, Proposal, Midterm Review, Defense), clearly highlighting the user's current stage to provide strong navigational context. Concerning the lack of clear return paths on detail pages, a consistent left-aligned 'Back' chevron icon will be mandated across all deep-level views. This will be complemented by a Breadcrumb Navigation on complex pages to constantly show the user their path hierarchy. Finally, ambiguous interactions, such as document submission, will be immediately confirmed via a Toast/Snackbar notification (e.g., "Submission Successful, Awaiting Instructor Review"), and the instructor communication interface will be updated to include a filterable conversation history log to strengthen consistency and predictability.

Category C - Visual Layout & Consistency

Issues related to inconsistent spacing, alignment, and icon usage primarily stem from a lack of a rigorous design specification. The solution involves establishing and strictly adhering to a Grid and Spacing System using a base unit for all vertical and horizontal spacing. All textual elements will utilize a uniform left-alignment standard. Critically, we will conduct an audit and update the Iconography Library to ensure all icons share a consistent visual style, weight, and size, and that their associated labels are semantically accurate. By enforcing these visual standards, we will eliminate distractions, improve readability, and elevate the perceived professionalism of the wireframe.

四、 Expected Improvements After Addressing Problems

Improvement from Category A: Enhanced Comprehension and Onboarding Success

By fixing the information architecture flaws, the system will achieve a significantly lower cognitive load. The clear visual hierarchy and card-based modules will allow users to locate critical information 20% faster. The addition of introductory guides and explicit CTAs will boost the first-time user task completion rate by increasing confidence and drastically reducing instances of user confusion or hesitancy. The system will become more intuitive and immediately usable, requiring minimal external instruction.

Improvement from Category B: Strong Predictability and Flow Integrity

The integration of visual process maps and consistent navigation will eliminate user anxiety related to their progress. The Multi-Step Stepper will act as a perpetual guide, confirming the user's academic status. Reducing user disorientation and "pogo-sticking" (bouncing between pages) will be a key metric of success. Clear return paths and immediate action confirmations will transform the complex academic workflow into a smooth, predictable, and fully supported user journey, enhancing overall system reliability and perceived control.

Improvement from Category C: Elevated Professionalism and Trust

Adopting a unified visual design system will immediately elevate the wireframe's aesthetics and perceived credibility. By ensuring consistent spacing and alignment across all pages, we will create a clean, coherent, and professional interface. This level of visual polish directly impacts user trust and focus, allowing users to concentrate on their academic tasks rather than being distracted by inconsistent presentation. The result is a more reliable and polished final product that meets high professional standards.
